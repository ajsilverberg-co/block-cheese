## Abstract

Commerce on the Internet has come to rely almost exclusively on financial
institutions serving as trusted third parties. Cheese, similarly, has come to
rely on cheesemakers serving as trusted aging parties. Both systems suffer
from the inherent weaknesses of the trust-based model: transactions can be
reversed, and cheddar can be pre-shredded.

Block Cheese proposes a solution to the double-melt problem using a
peer-to-peer network of temperature-controlled caves. Instead of mining,
transactions are aged to perfection. The more time passes, the more valuable
your assets become — unless they go bad.

Full details, tokenomics, and governance structure are in the
[whitepaper](./block_cheese_wheytepaper.pdf) (the "Wheytepaper").

## How it works

| Legacy term | Block Cheese term |
|---|---|
| Block | Wheel |
| Transaction | Slice |
| Gas fee | Whey |
| Sharding | Curdling |
| Layer 2 | Fondue |
| Halving | Slicing |
| 51% attack | Cutting the Cheese |

Consensus is achieved via **Proof of Age**: validators ("affineurs") stake
wheels in cold storage. The longer a wheel ages, the sharper — and more
valuable — it becomes. Wheels left unattended spoil and are burned by the
protocol. This is a feature.

## Risk Disclosure

This token has no utility, no intrinsic value, and contains no cheese. It may
go bad; that is the entire premise. Past aging is no guarantee of future
flavor. Not FDIC, USDA, or fromagerie insured. Nothing in this repository is
financial, legal, or dietary advice.
